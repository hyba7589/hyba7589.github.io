Overview

Queen is a popular rock band that formed in 1970. They formed in London, England. Their vocalist was Freddie Mercury, their guitarist was Brian May, and their drummer was Roger Taylor. Brian May and Roger Taylor also served as backing vocalists. They had several different bassists before they stuck with John Deacon. They are best known for their massive hit songs, including Bohemian Rhapsody, We Will Rock You, Don't Stop Me Now, Killer Queen, and many more. The band went on until 1991, when singer Freddie Mercury tragically passed away from bronchopneumonia while suffering from AIDS. To this day, they are one of the best selling bands in the world. Their legacy lives on with Brian May and Roger Taylor now performing with singer Adam Lambert as Queen+Adam Lambert. Many musicians around the world often cite Queen as one of their influences, across all genres from pop to metal.

Albums

  • Queen(1973)
    •

    This was Queen's first album. This album was more in the progressive rock style, and had 3 singles: Keep Yourself Alive, Liar, and instrumental track Seven Seas of Rye. While not as successful as their later works, the album sold over 500k copies worldwide.

  • Queen II(1974)
    •

    This was Queen's second album. This album branched out in glam rock and hard rock genres, and had the only single was the new version of Seven Seas of Rye, now with vocals. The album was not very successful, as it only sold over 300k copies worldwide. copies.

  • Sheer Heart Attack(1974)
    •

    This was Queen's third album. The main genres in this one were hard rock and glam rock. It had two singles, one being the ever popular hit Killer Queen, with the other being Now I'm Here. Other noteable and well known tracks include Brighton Rock, Stone Cold Crazy, and Flick of the Wrist. The album was pretty successful, as it sold over a million copies worldwide.

  • A Night At The Opera(1975)
    •

    This was Queen's fourth album. This album branched out in genres. While some songs were in past genres performed by Queen, such as glam rock and hard rock, it also has some pop influence. It had two singles, one being the world renowned hit Bohemian Rhapsody, with the other being the popular hit You're My Best Friend. Other noteable and well known tracks include Death on Two Legs, I'm in Love with my Car, and Love of my Life. The album was very successful, as it sold over four million copies worldwide.

  • A Day at the Races(1976)
    •

    This was Queen's fifth album. This album was also in more of the rock and pop genres, but also dove into symphonies. It had five singles, with them being Somebody to Love, Tie Your Mother Down, Teo Torriatte (Let Us Cling Together), Good Old-Fashioned Lover Boy, and Long Away. While not matching the past album's success, the album was still successful, as it sold over 1.5 million copies worldwide.

  • News of the World(1977)
    •

    This was Queen's sixth album. This album was mainly in the hard rock and arena rock genres. It had four singles. Two of them were the often back-to-back performed sensations of We Will Rock You and We Are the Champions, while the other two were Spread Your Wings and It's Late. The album was very successful, as it sold over five million copies worldwide.

  • Jazz(1978)
    •

    This was Queen's seventh album. This album added onto the past album's genres with it also being pop and metal. The album had five singles. Two were the cross-referenced hits of Fat Bottomed Girls and Bicycle Race, and another one was the sensation Don't Stop Me Now. The other two singles were Mustapha and Jealousy. The album was successful, as it sold over two million copies worldwide.

  • The Game(1980)
    •

    This was Queen's eighth album. This album explored the funk rock and disco rock genres, while also having some electronic music influence. This album had five singles. Two of them were the massive hits Another One Bites the Dust and Crazy Little Thing Called Love. The other three singles were Save Me, Play the Game, and Need Your Loving Tonight. The album was very successful, as it sold over five million copies worldwide.

  • Flash Gordon(1980)
    •

    This was Queen's ninhth album, as well as a soundtrack album. It was in the rock genre. Due to it being a soundtrack album, the only single on it was Flash, with the other songs not being very well known. It only sold over 200k copies worldwide.

  • Hot Space(1982)
    •

    This was Queen's tenth album. It explored many genres, like pop rock, new wave, dance, disco, funk, and R&B. It had six singles. Five of them were the lesser known songs Body Language, Las Palabras de Amor, Calling All Girls, Staying Power, and Back Chat. The album also had the sensation Under Pressure, a collaboration with rockstar David Bowie. The album was somewhat successful, as it sold over 700k copies worldwide.

  • The Works(1984)
    •

    This was Queen's eleventh album. It explored several different rock genres as well as synth-pop and electro pop. It had four singles. Three of them were the massive hits Radio Ga Ga, I Want to Break Free, and Hammer to Fall. The other one was It's a Hard Life. Another noteable track on the album was Is This The World We Created...? The album was pretty successful, as it sold over 2 million copies worldwide.

  • A Kind of Magic(1986)
    •

    This was Queen's twelfth album. It was in the hard rock and pop rock genres. It had the most singles of any Queen album, with seven. These seven singles were One Vision, Princes of the Universe, A Kind of Magic, Friends Will Be Friends, Pain Is So Close to Pleasure, Who Wants to Live Forever, and One Year of Love. The album was pretty successful, as it sold over 2 million copies worldwide.

  • The Works(1989)
    •

    This was Queen's thirteenth album. It was in the rock and pop genres. It had five singles. These singles include the massively successful I Want It All and The Invisible Man, as well as Breakthru, Scandal, and The Miracle. The album was fairly successful, as it sold over 1.5 million copies worldwide.

  • Innuendo(1991)
    •

    This was Queen's fourteenth album, and the last one released during Freddie Mercury's lifetime. It was in the hard rock genre. It had five singles. Four were the massively successful hits The Show Must Go On, I'm Going Slightly Mad, These Are the Days of Our Lives, and Innuendo. Another single was Headlong. The album was pretty successful, as it sold over 2 million copies worldwide.

  • Made in Heaven(posthumous, 1995)
    •

    This was Queen's fifteenth album, as well as their final album. It was in the pop and rock genres. It had six singles. These songs were Heaven for Everyone, A Winter's Tale, Too Much Love Will Kill You, I Was Born to Love You, Let Me Live, and You Don't Fool Me. The album was massively successful, as it sold over 6 million copies worldwide.
